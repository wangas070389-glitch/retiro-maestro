# RED-014: Analysis of Age-Based Projection Inaccuracies & Employment Gaps

## 1. Vulnerability: Projection Drift
**Risk:** High
**Description:** As the simulation age decreases (e.g., a 40-year-old), the uncertainty of legal anchors (UMA, Inflation) and individual data (Salary) increases.
**Mitigation:** Reports for users < 55 must include a "High Sensitivity" warning and emphasize that projections are based on current laws which are subject to sovereign reform.

## 2. Threat: The "Rights Decay" Loophole
**Actor:** Uninformed User
**Vector:** A user marked as "Desempleado" might assume their pension is safe, while in reality, they might lose "Conservación de Derechos" before reaching 60.
**Mitigation:** The engine must check for specialized rights conservation (usually 1/4 of total cotization weeks) and flag a warning if the "AS-IS" gap exceeds this limit.

## 3. Data Poisoning: Employment Status Injection
**Risk:** Medium
**Description:** Incorrectly toggling "Employed" on a long-term unemployed profile can lead to "hallucinated" weeks in the report.
**Mitigation:** Add a confirmation prompt when switching to "Employed" if the last known registration date is significantly in the past.
